Water Testing Services

Subsections:

  • Drinking Water Quality Testing
  • Wastewater and Effluent Analysis
  • Surface and Groundwater Monitoring
    •

The Environmental Analysis and Consultancy Unit (EACU) offers comprehensive testing of potable, industrial, and environmental water samples. We adhere to national (LIBNOR) and international standards to ensure safety and compliance of drinking water and other water sources.

Common tests include:

  • Physicochemical parameters: pH, electrical conductivity (EC), total dissolved solids (TDS), turbidity, hardness (total, calcium, magnesium), alkalinity (total, carbonate, bicarbonate), color, odor
  • Heavy metals: Sodium (Na), Potassium (K), Lead (Pb), Cadmium (Cd), Arsenic (As), Chromium (Cr), Iron (Fe), Zinc (Zn), Copper (Cu), Manganese (Mn), etc. (using AAS, Flame Photometer)
  • Nutrients and inorganic ions:Nitrate (NO₃⁻), Nitrite (NO₂⁻), Ammonia (NH₃), Phosphate (PO₄³⁻), Chloride, Sulfate (analyzed by UV-Vis, Titration)
  • Microbiological indicators:Total Coliforms, Escherichia coli (E. coli)
  • Organic and inorganic pollutants: BOD₅ (5‑day incubation), COD (UV‑Vis), Dissolved Oxygen (DO)
  • Suspended solids: Total solids (TS), TSS
  • Sediment composition and thermal stability (TGA)

  • Agricultural Soil Testing
  • Contaminated Site Assessment

The Environmental Analysis and Consultancy Unit (EACU) provides comprehensive physical and chemical analyses of soils and sediments. Our testing supports assessment of soil fertility, contamination levels, and suitability for agricultural or developmental purposes.

Common tests include:

Instrumental Analysis Services

Subsections:

  • Advanced Material and Chemical Characterization
  • Elemental and Spectroscopic Analysis
  • Chromatographic Analysis and Molecular Diagnostics
    •

The Environmental Analysis and Consultancy Unit (EACU) provides access to a wide array of advanced instrumental techniques, supporting academic research, regulatory compliance, product quality assurance, and environmental assessments. Our services are designed for the accurate identification, quantification, and characterization of diverse sample types, including environmental, industrial, agricultural, and food matrices.

Available techniques include

  • Mass Spectrometry (MS):

    For detection of volatile organic compounds (VOCs), pesticides residues, hydrocarbons, and residual solvents

  • High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC)::

    Ideal for analyzing pharmaceuticals, organic acids, preservatives, dyes, and water-soluble compounds.

  • Fourier-Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR):

    Functional group identification and material fingerprinting of organic compounds, polymers, and unknown substances.

  • Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA):

    Evaluates thermal stability, decomposition behavior, and moisture/volatile content in solids and polymers.

  • Elemental Analyzer (CHNS):

    Accurate quantification of carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, and sulfur (CHNS) in environmental or food samples.

  • AAS (Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy):

    Precise detection of trace metals (e.g., Pb, Cd, Zn, Fe, Cr, Mn, Cu) in soil, water, fertilizers, and food.

  • UV-Vis Spectrophotometry:

    Routine analysis of nutrients, color, and other chemical species.

  • Kjeldahl Nitrogen Analysis:

    Determination of total nitrogen in fertilizers, food, and biological materials

Instrumentation Capabilities

HPLC

High-Performance Liquid Chromatography A separation technique for identifying, quantifying, and purifying individual components in a mixture.
  • Detection of preservatives and additives in food
  • Drug analysis
  • Mycotoxin detection
  • Organic acid analysis in fertilizers

AAS

Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometer Measures the concentration of metal ions in samples.
  • Heavy metals in water and food (Pb, Cd, Cr, etc.)
  • Trace elements in fertilizers and soil
  • Toxic metal residues in wastewater

UV-Vis Spectrophotometer

Uses ultraviolet and visible light to analyze absorbance.
  • Determination of BOD, COD, ammonia, phosphate
  • Color intensity in food and water
  • Quantification of pigments, dyes, and phenols

Elemental Analyzer

Determines the elemental composition (C, H, N, S, O) in organic and inorganic materials.
  • Organic matter in soil and fertilizers
  • Protein and carbon content in food
  • Total nitrogen for Kjeldahl comparisons

Kjeldahl Nitrogen Analyzer

Measures nitrogen content via digestion and titration.
  • Total nitrogen in fertilizers
  • Protein estimation in food
  • Soil fertility analysis

Flame Photometer

Measures concentration of alkali and alkaline earth metals.
  • Sodium, potassium, calcium analysis in water and fertilizers
  • Salinity checks

TGA

Thermogravimetric Analyzer Measures weight changes in materials as a function of temperature.
  • Moisture and volatile matter in fertilizers
  • Thermal stability of materials
  • Organic content in solids

FTIR

Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy Identifies organic and some inorganic compounds based on IR absorption.
  • Functional group identification in complex samples
  • Polymer and contaminant identification
  • Structural analysis of agricultural inputs

Sample Submission & Forms

At the Environmental Analysis & Consultancy Unit (EACU), we are dedicated to ensuring that every stage of the sample submission process is clear, efficient, and reliable.

Our services cover water, wastewater, soil, fertilizer, and food analyses, and each sample received is handled under strict quality control procedures to ensure accuracy, traceability, and validity of results.

To help us deliver dependable and scientifically sound outcomes, please follow the submission instructions below.

  Step 1: Complete the Submission Form

  • Download and fill out the EACU Sample Submission Form.

  • Provide complete and accurate information, including:
    • Client and contact details
    • Sample identification and description
    • Requested analyses and parameters
    • Signature and declaration of sample authenticity

  • Incomplete forms or missing information may result in processing delays

  Step 2: Sample Collection & Labeling

Proper collection and labeling are critical for valid analytical results.

  • Each sample should be clearly labeled with:
    • A unique Sample ID or reference number
    • Date & time of collection
    • Sampling location/site
    • Collector's name

  • Use clean, uncontaminated containers:
    • Containers should be appropriate for the type of analysis.
    • Rinse or pre-wash them if required, unless they are pre-preserved.
    • Fill the container to the recommended volume; leave headspace when specified (for certain water tests such as BOD).
    • Avoid touching the inner surfaces of containers or lids; use clean gloves during handling.

  • Minimum sample volume requirements:

    • To ensure sufficient volume for analysis, quality control, and potential re-testing, please provide at least:

    • Water and wastewater samples: 1.0 liter
    • Soil and fertilizer samples: 250 grams
    • Food samples: 200 grams (or equivalent volume)
    • Other matrices (upon request): as specified by EACU before sampling

    Submitting less than the minimum volume may prevent completion of all requested analyses or quality checks.

  Step 3: Preservation, Holding Time & Storage

To maintain the representativeness and stability of your samples:


  • Apply proper preservatives where required (e.g. acidification, chemical stabilizers) for different test types.
  • Keep samples refrigerated at 4 - 6 °C unless otherwise instructed.
  • Do not freeze samples unless specifically instructed.
  • Observe the maximum holding times for different analyses:
    • Microbiological tests: within 6 - 24 hours
    • Nutrients and metals analyses: within a few days (depending on preservative used)

  Step 4: Safe Transportation

To ensure integrity during transport:

  • Ensure all containers are tightly sealed to prevent leakage.
  • Use insulated coolers or ice packs for temperature-sensitive samples.
  • Avoid exposure to heat, sunlight, or excessive agitation during transport.
  • Include the completed submission form and any chain-of-custody records with the samples.
  • Notify the laboratory in advance of large or special shipments to ensure proper receipt and handling.
    • Samples should arrive at the laboratory within the acceptable temperature range (≤ 6 °C), unless otherwise instructed. Samples received above this limit may be rejected or qualified in the report.

  Step 5: Submission Deadlines & Acceptance

  • Certain analyses - particularly microbiological tests - are highly time-sensitive and must be delivered promptly.
  • Samples received beyond the maximum holding time may not be accepted for testing.
  • For bulk submissions or research projects, please coordinate with the laboratory prior to delivery to ensure timely processing.

  Turnaround Time

  • The standard reporting time for most analyses is 10 working days from receipt of the sample, unless otherwise agreed.
  • Extended turnaround times may apply for specialized, confirmatory, or microbiological analyses.
  • The expected report delivery date will be confirmed at the time of sample submission.
  • Express services may be available upon request and prior approval.

Sample Retention and Disposal

Unless otherwise requested in writing, samples are retained under controlled conditions for a minimum of seven (7) days after reporting, after which they are disposed of according to environmental and safety regulations.

Quality Assurance

All samples received at EACU are processed under strict quality management procedures to ensure data reliability, traceability, and compliance with recognized testing standards. Clients are encouraged to maintain proper documentation throughout the collection and submission process to uphold the integrity of results.
